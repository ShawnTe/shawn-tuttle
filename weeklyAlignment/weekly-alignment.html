If this sounds familiar…

  • You start Monday with the best intentions—and by Wednesday the week has already happened to you.
  • Your to-do list keeps growing, but the things that actually matter barely move.
  • You spend energy on what feels urgent and keep deferring what feels important.
  • By Friday you feel like you worked hard all week but can’t quite point to what you accomplished.
  • You’ve tried planning tools and better systems. Some help for a while. But the drift keeps coming back.

The issue isn’t that you aren’t capable or that you need more willpower. It’s that you aren’t aligning with the realities of your week.

This is what The Weekly Alignment is for.

A simple, guided weekly process—live, together, once a week. The same structure each time, so the practice builds without you having to figure anything out.

  • 01

    Orient

    Get a clear view of what’s in play this week.

    • Take stock of your schedule and commitments
    • Notice what’s already requiring bandwidth
    • Get clear on what needs your focus now
  • 02

    Align

    Shape a week that fits your capacity.

    • Decide what this week is about
    • Shape your week around it
    • Set yourself up to follow through
  • 03

    Navigate

    Prepare for the moments that usually throw you off.

    • Recognize the kinds of friction you tend to run into
    • Decide how you’ll respond instead of avoiding
    • Keep things moving without losing yourself in the process

Here’s what starts to change…

When you have a live, weekly space to reset and align, the week stops happening to you.

  • →Monday feels different. You start clear on what matters—and with a realistic plan to match.
  • →The mental load of “figuring out the week” gets lifted. That work gets done in the session.
  • →Urgent things still happen. They just stop consuming everything.
  • →You stop drifting and start steering.
  • →You catch yourself going off-course sooner—and you know how to return.
  • →By the end of the month, you trust yourself more. Because you’ve been following through.

You can do this on your own.

There are journals, planners, apps, and YouTube videos about weekly reviews. They work for some people, some of the time.

But here’s what most of them can’t give you: a live space where showing up is the practice.

“This is when I do that.”

When you’re in a room—even a virtual one—with someone guiding the process, something shifts. You actually pause. You actually think. You don’t skip it because it got busy or because you’re not in the mood.

The structure is already there. The questions are already prepared. You just have to arrive.
